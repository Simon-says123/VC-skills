# IC Competitive Checklist

Pre-flight self-review before presenting the draft at Gate 2.
Work through every question. If you cannot answer one confidently, flag it in the report or resolve it with additional research.

---

## Section 1 — Competitive Map completeness

- [ ] Have I identified direct competitors, indirect competitors, AND substitutes?
- [ ] Is the competitor set sourced from independent research, not just the founder's deck?
- [ ] Are all funding figures sourced from public announcements (not estimated)?
- [ ] Have I checked whether any competitor backer could conflict with IAG's CVC participation?
- [ ] If the founder claimed zero competition, have I explained in the report what I found and what that claim signals?
- [ ] Is the table sorted by funding within each tier?
- [ ] Have I flagged any competitor that raised funding in the past 12 months?

---

## Section 2 — Differentiation Analysis quality

- [ ] Have I distinguished execution advantage (temporary) from structural differentiation (durable)?
- [ ] Have I stress-tested founder-claimed differentiation against what customers actually pay for — not what the deck asserts?
- [ ] If three or more well-funded players are at similar stages, have I explicitly named the margin compression risk?
- [ ] Have I assessed asymmetric competitive dynamics if the startup is competing against manual processes or Excel?

---

## Section 3 — Moat Assessment rigour

- [ ] Have I scored all six moat types, not just the obvious ones?
- [ ] Have I applied the lock-in test: does each claimed moat compound over time?
- [ ] Have I flagged compliance certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001) that were cited as moats but are actually table stakes?
- [ ] Have I been honest about weak or absent moats rather than hedging?
- [ ] Is my scoring consistent with the criteria in `references/moat-framework.md`?

---

## Section 4 — Competitive Risks coverage

- [ ] Have I covered the incumbent acceleration scenario?
- [ ] Have I covered the best-capitalised peer raising a large round and compressing pricing?
- [ ] Is the "What could kill this?" scenario specific and credible — not generic category risk?
- [ ] If competing against manual processes: have I framed inertia and procurement cycles as the actual risk?

---

## Section 5 — IC Verdict readiness

- [ ] Does the IC Verdict state a clear position (green light / yellow flag / red flag)?
- [ ] Is the verdict supported by specific evidence from the report — not just assertions?
- [ ] If an anchor question was provided at Gate 1, is it directly answered?
- [ ] Is the verdict written in "we" / "us" voice consistent with IAGFV style?
- [ ] Would an IC member reading only the Executive Verdict and IC Verdict understand the competitive risk profile without reading the rest?

---

## Sources quality

- [ ] Are at least 15 sources listed?
- [ ] Do sources prioritise analysis articles, research reports, and industry commentary over company websites?
- [ ] Does each source have a 1-sentence note explaining why it's worth Simon reading?
- [ ] Are all source URLs live and not paywalled where possible?

---

## Questions the IC is likely to raise

These are the competitive questions that come up repeatedly at IAGFV investment committee. If the report does not address them, add coverage before finalising.

1. "Who is the most dangerous competitor right now — and why haven't they already won?"
2. "What happens to this company's position if [named incumbent] acquires [named competitor]?"
3. "Is the moat real or is this just first-mover advantage dressed up in moat language?"
4. "If the startup loses its biggest customer to a competitor tomorrow, what does that tell us?"
5. "Is competitive position improving or degrading as the category matures?"
6. "What does this company need to be true for its differentiation to still hold in 3 years?"
7. "Are any of the competitors backed by an insurer or reinsurer we co-invest with?"
8. "How does the startup's pricing compare — and is pricing a competitive weapon or a vulnerability?"
9. "What's the realistic M&A scenario — acquirer or acquiree? Does that change the competitive calculus?"
10. "If we pass on this deal, could a competitor use our LP network to fund this startup's rival?"
