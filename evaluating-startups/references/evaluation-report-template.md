# Evaluation Report Template

**Type:** Internal research document — deep-dive companion to the Opportunity Brief  
**Audience:** Ventures team internal use; not for direct sharing with founders or external parties  
**Purpose:** Capture the full research base for a startup entering deeper evaluation. This is the evidence layer behind the Opportunity Brief — more detailed, more analytical, and less polished. It is the working document that makes the brief credible.  
**Target length:** 6 pages when rendered as PDF  
**Stage:** Post-initial screening, pre-IC discussion

---

## Section 0 — Executive Summary

**Format:** 3–4 paragraphs + clear recommendation

This section is written last, after all other sections are complete. It distills the full evaluation into a concise, opinionated summary for the ventures team.

**Paragraph 1 — What the company does:**
1–2 sentences on the business: product, market, stage, and sourcing context.

**Paragraph 2 — The bull case:**
2–3 sentences summarising the strongest signals across team, product, traction, and market. Prioritise the 2–3 factors that most differentiate this company.

**Paragraph 3 — The bear case:**
2–3 sentences summarising the most significant risks or gaps. Be direct — do not soften concerns.

**Paragraph 4 — Strategic fit:**
1–2 sentences on how this connects to the ventures team's mandate: which BU, which KPI, what mechanism.

**Recommendation:**

> **[Proceed / Stop / Conditional proceed]**
>
> One sentence justifying the recommendation. If conditional, state what would need to be true to move forward.

**Standard:** The recommendation must be a clear directional call. "More research needed" is not a recommendation. If the data is genuinely insufficient to call it, state that explicitly and specify what is missing.

## Section 2 — TAM (Market Analysis)

**Format:** 3–4 paragraphs + key data points

Cover:
- TAM, SAM, and initial beachhead market with sources
- Primary growth drivers (regulatory, technology, demographic, structural)
- Timing thesis: why now, and what has changed in the last 2–3 years
- Geographic scope and expansion logic

**Key data points to source:**
- Market size estimate (cite source and year)
- Projected CAGR (cite source)
- Regulatory or policy tailwinds relevant to this market
- Any incumbent market share data available

## Section 3 — Tech (Product and Technology)

**Format:** 3–4 paragraphs

- Where it sits in the technology stack (infrastructure, platform, application layer)

## Labelling Standards

- **[Inference]** — logically derived from available evidence, not directly stated in a source
- **[Unverified]** — could not be confirmed from public sources; requires data room, founder, or insider confirmation
- **[Speculation]** — an educated guess with limited evidential basis

All financial metrics, customer names, and funding data should be sourced and linked where possible.

---

## Formatting Rules

- Voice: third person (the company, the team, the founders) — this is a research document, not a pitch
- Length: 5 pages when rendered to PDF
- Tone: analytical and direct. State conclusions, not just observations
- No em dashes
- Label all gaps explicitly rather than leaving sections blank
