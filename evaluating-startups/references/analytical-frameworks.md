# Analytical Frameworks

**Purpose:** Five frameworks for structured analysis during startup evaluation. Apply 2–3 most relevant to the company's stage, category, and the key questions at hand. Do not run all five — select deliberately based on where the analytical gaps are.

---

## Framework 1 — Porter's Five Forces

**Use when:** The company operates in a competitive category and understanding structural market dynamics is critical to assessing moat durability.

**The five forces:**

| Force | What to assess |
|-------|---------------|
| Threat of new entrants | How easy is it to enter this market? What are the barriers (capital, data, regulation, switching costs)? |
| Bargaining power of buyers | How concentrated are customers? Can they push pricing down or switch easily? |
| Bargaining power of suppliers | Do key suppliers (data, infrastructure, API providers) have pricing power over the company? |
| Threat of substitutes | Can customers solve the same problem a different way — including doing nothing or building internally? |
| Competitive rivalry | How intense is existing competition? What is the basis of competition (price, product, distribution)? |

**Output:** A brief assessment of each force (high / medium / low pressure) with a 1–2 sentence rationale. Conclude with: what does the overall structure tell us about the attractiveness and defensibility of this position?

## Framework 2 — Problem-Solution Fit

**Use when:** The company is early stage, the market is unproven, or there is ambiguity about whether the product is solving a real, urgent problem for a specific customer segment.

**Key questions:**

1. **Problem clarity:** Is the problem well-defined, consistently described by customers, and genuinely painful enough to pay to solve?
2. **Customer segment specificity:** Who exactly experiences this problem — not "enterprises" but which buyer, in which industry, at which size?
3. **Existing solutions:** What do customers do today? Why is that insufficient?
4. **Solution fit:** Does the product directly address the core pain, or is it a partial or adjacent solution?
5. **Willingness to pay:** Is there evidence that customers will pay — and at what price point?
6. **Adoption signals:** Are early customers actually using the product, or is adoption shallow post-purchase?

**Output:** A 3–4 sentence summary of problem-solution fit quality. Flag whether the fit is validated (paid, live customers with strong engagement), early-stage (LOIs, pilots, positive qualitative signals), or unproven (limited evidence, assumption-heavy).

## Framework 3 — Unit Economics Analysis

**Use when:** There are questions about the business model's long-term viability, margin structure, or whether the GTM motion is economically sustainable.

**Metrics to derive or estimate:**

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-----------------|
| Gross margin | Revenue quality and scalability of the core product |
| LTV (Customer Lifetime Value) | Total expected revenue from a customer over their tenure |
| CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) | Cost to acquire a new customer across all sales and marketing spend |
| LTV/CAC ratio | Rule of thumb: 3x+ is healthy; below 1x is structurally broken |
| Payback period | How long to recover CAC from gross profit; under 18 months is strong |
| NRR (Net Revenue Retention) | Revenue expansion within existing accounts; above 110% is excellent |
| Burn multiple | Net burn / net new ARR — measures efficiency of growth spend |

**Output:** Estimate or source each metric where possible. For any metric not publicly available, note it as [Unverified] and flag as a question for data room or follow-up conversation. Conclude with: does the unit economics profile suggest a capital-efficient, scalable model, or does it raise questions about long-term viability?

## Framework 4 — Technology and Moat Assessment

**Use when:** The company's differentiation claim is technology-based and the durability of that advantage needs stress-testing.

**Assessment dimensions:**

| Dimension | Questions to answer |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Proprietary vs. commodity technology | Is the core technology built in-house, or assembled from open-source and third-party components? |
| Data advantage | Does the company have a proprietary dataset that is hard to replicate and compounds over time? |
| Network effects | Does the product get more valuable as more users or data points are added? |
| Switching costs | How difficult or costly is it for a customer to switch to a competitor once they have integrated? |
| IP protection | Are there patents, trade secrets, or regulatory approvals that create formal barriers? |
| Replication timeline | How long would it take a well-resourced competitor to build equivalent capability? |

**Output:** Rate each dimension as Strong, Moderate, Weak, or Not applicable. Summarise in 2–3 sentences: what is the primary source of competitive advantage and how durable is it over a 3–5 year horizon?

## Framework 5 — Insurance Value Chain Mapping

**Use when:** Assessing where in the insurance stack the company creates value and how directly it connects to IAG's core business.

**The insurance value chain:**

```
Distribution → Underwriting → Policy Admin → Claims → Customer Experience
      ↑                                                         ↑
 Customer Acquisition                                    Retention / Renewal
```

**Assessment questions:**

1. **Where does the company sit?** Map to one or more stages of the value chain above.
2. **What is the mechanism?** Does the company reduce costs (efficiency), improve outcomes (quality), or enable new products or markets (growth)?
3. **Which IAG business unit benefits?** Map to specific BUs: RIA (Retail Insurance Australia), IIA (Intermediated Insurance Australia), NZ (New Zealand), Group.
4. **Which IAG KPI does it move?** Loss ratio, GWP, expense ratio, COR, NPS, customer retention.
5. **What is the integration path?** API integration, data sharing, white-labelling, co-development, or distribution partnership?
6. **What is the pilot readiness?** Could a time-boxed pilot with a specific BU start within 6–12 months?
7. **Carrier benchmarking:** Are other insurers already using this — and if so, what does that signal about adoption risk and competitive urgency?

**Output:** A 3–4 sentence assessment of strategic relevance. Name specific BUs and KPIs. Conclude with a clear answer to: is this a strong strategic fit, a thin adjacency, or a longer-horizon option?

## Framework Selection Guide

| Situation | Recommended frameworks |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Early-stage, unproven market | Problem-Solution Fit + Insurance Value Chain Mapping |
| Competitive category, moat questions | Porter's Five Forces + Technology and Moat Assessment |
| Business model or scalability questions | Unit Economics Analysis + Porter's Five Forces |
| Insurance-specific regulatory or carrier context | Insurance Value Chain Mapping + Problem-Solution Fit |
| Mid-stage company with traction | Unit Economics Analysis + Technology and Moat Assessment + Porter's Five Forces |
